Our Strategy

Lab Development Roadmap

The GCI Lab executes a strategic two-phase implementation approach designed to maximize immediate research impact while securing long-term sustainability.

1

Phase 1: Virtual Lab Current Active Phase

Focusing on conceptual work, software development, cloud platforms (AWS, Google Cloud), open-source tools, and simulated environments to ensure rapid initiation.

2

Phase 2: Physical Infrastructure

Future expansion to a physical space equipped with ARM/GPU servers, IoT sensor rigs, and renewable energy demonstration hardware.

Research Themes

The lab pursues integrated themes across computer science, engineering, and environmental sustainability.

Green Data Centers & Cloud

Load balancing and virtualization to reduce power; renewable energy integration for servers; carbon-aware scheduling and resource allocation algorithms.

Energy-Efficient IoT & Edge

Low-power sensor networks, NB-IoT for smart grids and smart healthcare; energy-harvesting devices for sustainable edge computing deployments.

Green Software & AI

Energy profiling tools for applications; lightweight Machine Learning models for edge devices; algorithms optimized specifically for low energy consumption.

E-waste & Circular ICT

Lifecycle analysis of IT equipment; smart sorting and AI-driven methodologies for recycling components (e.g., building on student projects like ElectroSortNet).

Cross-cutting Interdisciplinary Domains

Applying green IT in critical domains to bridge disciplines. This includes sustainable healthcare systems (WBANs), IoT for environmental and agricultural monitoring, and applying optimization algorithms to large scale national infrastructure problems to achieve broader UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
