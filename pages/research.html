Research Themes

The lab pursues integrated themes across computer science, engineering, and environmental sustainability.

Green Software Design

Energy profiling frameworks for compilation and execution; carbon-aware application scheduling; and algorithmic complexity optimization targeted specifically at reducing CPU and memory power footprints.

Green Hardware-based System Design

Low-power SoC architectures, power-gating strategies, and circuit-level energy harvesting optimization for sustainable edge infrastructure and renewable-integrated server deployments.

Green AI for Substainability

Carbon-efficient model training and inference pipelines; deep learning compression techniques such as quantization and pruning; and lightweight neural architectures optimized for constrained edge devices.

Green E-Waste Management

Quantitative lifecycle assessment (LCA) protocols for enterprise computing assets, alongside computer-vision-driven automated classification models (e.g., ElectroSortNet) for physical component recycling pipelines.

Green CPS Design

Energy-efficient Cyber-Physical Systems featuring power-aware closed-loop control loops, low-power sensor telemetry, and optimized NB-IoT communications protocols for smart grid utilities.

Green Interdisciplinary Domains

Deployment of green ICT architectures within healthcare delivery (Wireless Body Area Networks), precision environmental tracking, and multi-objective optimization algorithms mapped to UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
